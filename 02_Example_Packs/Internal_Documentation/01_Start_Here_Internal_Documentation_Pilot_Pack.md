# Start Here - Internal Documentation Pilot Pack

## Purpose
This pack supports a small, controlled pilot for AI-assisted internal document work. The pilot is not about replacing judgment or approval. It is about testing whether a simple operating method can improve speed and consistency in low-risk document tasks while keeping ownership, review, and evidence clear.

## Pilot scope
This pilot is designed for:
- internal documentation tasks
- one team or function
- a limited time period, usually 30 days
- low-risk, document-focused use cases

## In scope
Examples of acceptable pilot tasks:
- draft internal process notes
- draft or revise SOP text for review
- prepare meeting summaries
- prepare handoff notes
- summarise approved internal material
- create first-pass internal memos or guidance text

## Out of scope
Examples that should not be included without separate controls:
- HR decisions
- disciplinary decisions
- legal conclusions
- financial approvals
- client-facing release without review
- unrestricted use of personal, confidential, or regulated data

## How the pilot works
1. A task is defined.
2. A named person uses AI for an approved support step.
3. A human reviews the output.
4. The result is logged.
5. The approver accepts, rejects, or requests revision.
6. Evidence is retained.

## What success looks like
The pilot is successful if the organization can show that:
- the method is understandable
- people can follow it without heavy training
- ownership remains clear
- document quality is acceptable
- turnaround time improves or stays reasonable
- a basic evidence trail exists
- no major governance failure occurs

## What this pack contains
Use the documents in this order:
- Start Here
- Sponsor / Team Lead one-pager
- RACI
- Weekly scorecard
- AI use log
- Review and evidence guide
- User and reviewer support documents

## Operating note
This is a method pack. It presents a simple control layer around work that already exists. It does not require a complex technical implementation to begin.

# Review and Evidence Guide

## Purpose
This guide explains what evidence should exist for an AI-assisted task in the pilot. It is intentionally simple. The goal is not to create heavy paperwork. The goal is to make the work traceable.

## Minimum evidence for each task
Each completed task should have, at minimum:
- task description
- date
- named user
- tool used
- source material used
- output file or link
- reviewer name
- review result
- approver or status outcome

## Review questions
The reviewer should check:
1. Is the output factually and procedurally acceptable?
2. Does it stay inside the approved task boundary?
3. Is the tone and format fit for use?
4. Are any claims or references checked where needed?
5. Is anything missing, misleading, or too confident?
6. Should the output be revised, rejected, or escalated?

## Evidence retention
Keep enough evidence to answer these questions later:
- what was the task
- who used AI
- what material was used
- who reviewed it
- what decision was made
- what final version was released

## Sampling option
If pilot volume becomes large, the team may retain full evidence for all work or full evidence for high-value tasks plus a sample for lower-value tasks. This should be agreed in advance.

## Red flags
Escalate when:
- the source material is unclear
- the reviewer cannot verify the content
- the task appears to shape a formal decision
- restricted or personal data may have been used inappropriately
- approval or ownership is missing
